Physical Water Treatment

Public drinking water systems use different water treatment methods to provide safe drinking water for their communities. Physical water treatment systems often use steps that include sedimentation, filtration, and distillation.

Biological Methods

Biological methods of water treatment leverage natural biological processes to remove contaminants from water. These methods are particularly effective for treating organic matter and certain types of pollutants

Advanced Methods

Advanced methods of water treatment are designed to address a wide range of contaminants and achieve high levels of water purity. These methods often involve sophisticated technology and are used in applications where traditional treatment methods are insufficient .


Learn more

Coagulation and Flocculation

Coagulation and flocculation are critical processes in water treatment that work together to remove suspended solids, colloids, and other impurities from water. These processes enhance the removal of particles that are too small to settle out of suspension naturally.
